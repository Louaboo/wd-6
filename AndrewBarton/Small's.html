Small's

All this information is from purely buying the ship and not putting and modules on it. Usually the bigger the ship the more armor and shields. Also the costs are for only the base ship.

Sidewinder: You start with this ship. You can either start with extra module's that are better, or with extra cash. It is probably the weakest ship in the game, that can be bought and flown without a fighter bay. It is a Multipurpose ship. It has some of the smallest module's. It cost's 32,000 cerdits.

Eagle: Cost's 44,800 cerdits. Is the first combat ship that can be bought. It is the most maneuvarable ship in the game. Yet, has the weakest firepower of Combat ships. It has smaller module's than any other combat ship.

Hauler: The Hauler is a freighter, trade ship. It is the smallest freighter in the game. It is the most maneuvarable freighter in the game. It has the weakest firepower of any freighter. It cost's 52,720 cerdits.

Adder: The Adder is a multipurpose ship. It is the next multipurpose ship after the sidewinder. It is very weak in shields, armor, and firepower. It costs 87.808.

Imperial Eagle: The Imperial Eagle is the first faction baised ship you can own. It is the first Imperial ship. It costs 110,830. It is quite manuevarble. It is a combat ship. It has 2 small hardpoints, and 1 medium hardpoint.

Viper Mk3: The Viper Mk3 is the next combat ship after the Imperial Eagle. It costs 142,931. It is about as manuevarble as the Imperial Eagle. It has 2 small hardpoints, and 2 medium hardpoints. Which is one more than the Imperial Eagle.

Cobra Mk3: The Cobra Mk3 is the next multipurpose after the Adder. It is less manuevarble then the Adder. It has one more medium hardpoint than the Adder, at two. It also starts with more cargo and a larger jump range of 1.34 LY, at 10.46.

Viper Mk4: The Viper Mk4 is a direct upgrade from the Mk3. It is a combat ship. It has a jump range of 10.36 which is 2.69 more than the Mk3. It is less manuevarble than the Mk3. It has 144 more armor at 270, yet less shields at 103 which is 34 MJ less than Mk3.

Diamondback Scout: the Diamondback is a the first explorer, mixed with a combat. It costs 564,330 credits. It's maneuvre rating is an 8. It has horrible cargo. It has a pretty good speed and boost speed.

Cobra Mk4: The Cobra Mk4 is the upgrade to the Cobra Mk3. Yet, it is only available to those who bought the game the first year. It is a multipurpose ship like the Mk3. It has less jump range, speed, and maneuvability. Yet, it does have more shields and the same armor. It costs 745,610 credits. It also has an extra small hardpoint with 3 and the same medium at 2, as the Mk3.

Diamondback Explorer: It is a upgrade to the Scout with a few changes. It costs 1,894,760 credits. It has more armour and shields. Yet, less maneurability. It loses its 2 small hardpoints that the scout has, yet gains the first large hardpoint in the game. It keeps the 2 medium hardpoints. It has the largest jump range in the game.

Imperial Courier: It is the second imperial ship in the game. It is a multipurpose ship. It costs 2,542,931 credits. It has not the best jump range, cargo or armor. It has pretty good shields and maneuvrability. Not many players use this ship.

Vulture: It is a combat ship with quite some firepower. It has 2 large hardpoints. It costs 4,925,615 credits. It is the most expensive small ship and more expensive then 3 mediums; the Type 6, Keelback, and Asp Scout. It is extremely maneuvrable ship with a maneuvre rate of 9. It is a very good ship.
